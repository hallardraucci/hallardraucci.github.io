Why did I choose computer science?

As a little girl, my "dream job" was to become a video game designer. I loved playing video games and I was interested in how they were created. As I grew up, my dad enforced the idea that computers were going to one day rule the world and it is important to stay in a field involving them. He himself works in a Data Analytics team. When it came time to choose what high school I wanted to attend, I decided to go to a technical high school. I went to Platt Technical High School where I studied computer aided design and drafting as a trade. I gained experience in both AutoCAD and SolidWorks. My senior project was to construct a foosball table in SolidWorks and later in real life. Pictures of the progression of our project can be seen here. Learning this trade helped me realize that I wanted to remain in the engineering field. Once I reached college, I took my dad's advice and chose to study Computer Science and Engineering (CSE). Although this major does align with my "dream job", I no longer want to do this. I am not entirely sure what I want to do so here I am living day by day.

What do I like to do in my free time?

Whether it be swimming, running, or hiking I love being active. I aquired an interest in swimming freshman year of college. A friend of mine asked me if I would be interested in doing a triatholon with her. I had never swam competitively and couldn't even remember when I last rode a bike. I thought to myself, why not? We started training early by swimming and running during the week and going for bike rides on the weekends. After competing in the triatholon, I went out and bought myself a swim training book to follow. I have gained a passion in swimming, I find it to be fun and an easy way to get my mind off things. On the other hand, running has been one of my passions for a very long time. I joined the cross country team when I was in 8th grade. I continued doing cross country for all four years of high school. Unfortunately, every year our team had the minimum amount of girls we could have to compete: five. My senior year we only had four girls which disqualified us in every meet. This did not stop me, I continued to try my hardest to compete against myself and teammates. I earned varsity every year and became captain my senior year. Throughout high school I also did indoor and outdoor track. I did indoor freshman through junior year, while doing outdoor for all four years. My events ranged from 400, 800, 4x4, 4x8, to even the 1600 meters. Although I did cross country, I found my favorite event to be the 4x8 or the 800 meter. Now that I am in college I continue to be active for myself.

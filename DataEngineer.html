Operations Performance, Training & Integration IT Day-Ahead Energy Load Forecasting Project Data Engineer

December 2018 - January 2019

During my UConn Senior Design Project sponsored by POWWR, I was asked if I would be interested in interning there for winter break. I accepted the position of Data Engineer where I received experience with MS SQL Server, Google Cloud Platform, Google Big Query, Jira, Agile methodology, and VBA.

My mornings here started with a 9 AM scrum meeting where the team would gather in on a conference call with the other employees who worked in different states. The scrum master would go through the weekly sprint and ask each person what they would be doing for that day and whether or not there were any blockers. On Fridays, we also took the time too look at the quick win Friday board. These tickets were considered quick wins because they could be finished within a couple of hours.

I quickly became part of the team receiving tickets to complete. My first task to help me on my feet was to clean up and format two excel sheets that needed to be uploaded into the MS database. To improve this weekly process, I created a macro in VBA to automate the clean up and save the files in the proper format. The user simply needed to click a button located on an excel sheet where it would then prompt them to choose excel file(s) and the location they wished to save it. The macro would do the rest, saving the company time since they needed this done every week.

The rest of the internship consisted of me working on data anomoly detection (DAD) checks. This was done through dynamic programming in SQL. The tickets would contain a query that needed to be modified using open queries. This allowed faster execution of queries on specified linked servers. Once the queries were modified, I needed to create a procedure that I would store in the database. Lastly, using visual studio I was able to test them against several linked servers to ensure they were working properly. Only then would the procedures be pushed into production for continuous use. Although my internship here was short, the work I completed was meaningful and a great learning experience.
